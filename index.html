Indian Martial Arts Forms

Exploring the diverse traditions that shaped India's martial heritage

01

Kalaripayattu

Kerala • 3000+ Years

The mother of all martial arts. An ancient combat system that combines strikes, grappling, weaponry, and healing practices. Kalaripayattu represents the complete warrior tradition of Kerala.

02

Silambam

Tamil Nadu • Ancient

Traditional stick fighting art emphasizing speed, agility, and weapon mastery. Silambam practitioners develop lightning-fast reflexes and precise footwork through generations of refined techniques.

03

Gatka

Punjab • Sikh Tradition

Weapon-based martial art of the Sikhs, emphasizing spiritual discipline alongside combat skills. Gatka combines meditation, physical fitness, and traditional weapon training.

04

Mardani Khel

Maharashtra • Maratha

Armed martial art of the Maratha warriors, focusing on sword and shield combat. This art form played a crucial role in Maratha military history and continues to preserve warrior traditions.

05

Thoda

Himachal Pradesh • Archery

Traditional archery martial art practiced in the Himalayan regions. Thoda combines precision archery with ritualistic celebrations, maintaining ancient hunting and warfare traditions.

06

Paika Akhada

Odisha • Traditional

Ancient warrior training system of Odisha, combining martial arts with traditional dance forms. This art emphasizes flexibility, strength, and cultural expression through movement.
